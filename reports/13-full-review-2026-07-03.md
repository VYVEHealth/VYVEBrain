## Pass 3 — GDPR executors (export + erase), end-to-end [COMPLETE]

**Headline:** both the erasure gap AND a matching export gap are CONFIRMED CRITICAL by FK evidence — but PM-688 proved the erasure gap against the WRONG function. Correcting the record and hardening the finding.

**Correction to PM-688 §1.1:** PM-688 diffed `gdpr_erase_purge_subject(subject_email)` — a 16.6KB function with trigger-toggling and a 45-table explicit list. **That function is NOT wired to anything.** The live erase executor (`gdpr-erase-execute`, cron #22) calls a *different* function: `gdpr_erasure_purge(p_email)`, 3.9KB. Two near-identical purge functions exist; only the smaller is live. Right conclusion, wrong evidence. This pass reads the wired one.

**CRITICAL 1 — erasure leaves 16 member-identifiable tables behind (FK-confirmed).**
The wired `gdpr_erasure_purge` deletes by a hand-maintained ~44-table explicit list, relying on CASCADE FKs to sweep anything unlisted. FK check on the 20 unlisted member_email tables: only **4 have CASCADE FK to members** (`checkin_reactions`, `connect_checkins`, `replay_video_views`, `weekly_challenge_participation` — safe). The other **16 have NO FK to members at all** and are silently retained after a "completed" erasure:
`mind_moods`, `mind_burnout_checks`, `mind_activities`, `mind_fitness_log`, `mind_trackers`, `mind_recovery_actions`, `mind_recovery_log`, `daily_mood_checkins` (150 rows), `live_checkin_submissions`, `manual_step_estimates`, `member_prompt_responses`, `member_prompt_dismissals`, `movement_activities` (44 rows), `partner_memberships`, `session_live_views`, `affirmation_favourites`.
The **entire Mind pillar has no cascade safety net.** `gdpr_erasure_requests` shows 2 executed erasures → standing Article 17 failure with named subjects, on the most sensitive data category (mood/burnout/journaling/mental-fitness). Confirmed, not inferred.

**CRITICAL 2 — export omits the same tables AND makes a false completeness claim.**
`gdpr-export-execute` (cron #21, Article 15) uses its own independent hand-maintained `MEMBER_SCOPED_TABLES` (40 tables). It omits the same Mind-pillar + newer activity/connect tables. The bundled `_readme` states the file contains **"every record VYVE Health CIC holds against your email address."** It does not. Export has no cascade to save it (it's SELECT, not DELETE), so the omission is total. A member exercising Article 15 today receives a file that silently excludes all their mental-health data under a written "everything" promise. This is the more exposed of the two because the member sees the output and the false claim.

**Root cause (single):** TWO independent hand-maintained table lists (one in the purge SQL, one in the export EF), neither catalog-driven, both drifted identically as the Mind pillar + connect/activity tables shipped without being added to either. **Fix once, correctly:** a single `SECURITY DEFINER` function that enumerates member-scoped tables from `information_schema`/`pg_catalog` at runtime (every table with member_email/author/shared_by), feeding BOTH export and erase. Then: re-run erasure for the 2 already-processed subjects; add a CI guard failing when a new member-scoped table is absent from the enumeration's allow/deny map.

**PASS on everything else in both executors:** cron-secret gated (correct for cron), FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED pick-due, 3-attempt retry with terminal-failure alerting, signed 7d URLs, auth.users whitelist-sanitised on export, third-party purges (Stripe/Brevo/PostHog) best-effort non-aborting on erase, full audit receipts. The *machinery* is genuinely good; only the table coverage is wrong.

**§23 rule earned:** TWO purge functions exist (`gdpr_erasure_purge` = live, `gdpr_erase_purge_subject` = orphan). Any erasure fix MUST target `gdpr_erasure_purge` (the one the cron calls) — patching the orphan changes nothing. Verify the wired function via the executor's actual RPC name before editing.

## Pass 4 — Clinical / crisis-scan coverage [COMPLETE]

**Headline:** crisis detection exists and is well-built, but is wired to (at most) onboarding only. Every ongoing free-text disclosure surface has neither scanning nor signposting. A member can disclose suicidal ideation through the primary weekly check-in and the system replies with an AI wellness tip and alerts no-one. CRITICAL.

**`crisis-scan` itself — well-built (PASS on quality):** service-role gated, deterministic two-tier keyword matching (critical: suicide/self-harm/abuse explicit; concern: overwhelm/numbness/worthlessness), human-in-the-loop alert email with Samaritans 116 123 / Shout 85258 / 999 signposting and 4h (critical) / 24h (concern) reach-out guidance, doesn't alter member experience, doesn't use AI (deterministic, auditable). This is the right tool.

**CRITICAL — coverage. crisis-scan is caller-driven and almost nothing calls it.**
- Its own alert copy self-describes as *"the onboarding scanner … running inside the onboarding pipeline."* It scans whatever `fields` the caller passes.
- Grep of all committed EF sources: **zero** call crisis-scan (not even onboarding, in the staged mirror — it may be wired in live onboarding v116 which isn't source-mirrored, but it is provably absent everywhere else).
- `wellbeing-checkin` v66 (live weekly check-in, the primary ongoing disclosure surface) takes member `free_text`, sends it straight to Claude for a persona reflection, stores it, returns advice. **No crisis-scan call.** Confirmed by full body read.
- Frontend signposting (Samaritans/116 123/Shout) appears on only 3 pages: `VYVE_Health_Hub.html` (unlinked clinical staging), `mental-fitness.html`, `movement-plans.html`. The disclosure surfaces have **zero**: `wellbeing-checkin.html` (2 textareas, 0 signposting), `journal.html` (2 textareas, 0), `monthly-checkin.html` (9 textareas, 0), `connect-checkin.html` (1 textarea, 0), `mind.html` (0).

**Compounding — persona is not re-evaluated on distress at check-in.** `wellbeing-checkin` reads `members.persona` as-is. A low-wellbeing member assigned NOVA/SPARK (performance/motivational personas) writing distress in free_text gets a performance-framed AI reply, not a trauma-informed one. Only HAVEN's prompt contains crisis-signposting instruction, and HAVEN is not guaranteed and is itself un-signed-off (PM-688 §5.1). The check-in also pulls `sensitive_context` into the prompt ("lead with extra care") which is good, but that's a soft AI instruction, not a safeguarding trigger.

**Fix (Lewis + Phil own the clinical policy; Dean wires it):**
1. Call `crisis-scan` from `wellbeing-checkin`, `monthly-checkin`, journal save, daily-mood save, and any HAVEN turn — pass the free_text fields. It already returns fast and non-blocking.
2. Add persistent crisis signposting (Samaritans/Shout, on-asset) to every mental-health + free-text surface — this is the §23.84-class requirement, currently met on 3 pages, needed on ~8 more.
3. On a critical hit at check-in: consider suppressing the AI persona reply in favour of a fixed, clinically-approved crisis response, and force-route to HAVEN/RIVER. Policy call for Phil.
This is distinct from and more urgent than the PM-688 HAVEN-signoff finding: that's "un-reviewed persona live"; this is "no safety net on the disclosure surfaces at all."
